1. Pick the Right AI for the Job

Before you use AI, match the tool to the work in front of you.

Pick the Right AI for the Job

ChatGPT-style tools are language models, so they are useful for drafting emails, summaries, instructions, and meeting notes. Other AI tools are better for different inputs, such as images, audio, repeated issues, or trends over time.

  • Drafting emails and summaries: language models
  • Uploaded notes and reports: language models plus careful review
  • CSV files and repeated issues: pattern finding or classification
  • Screenshots and images: vision models
  • Object detection: detection models like YOLO
  • Object outlines and movement tracking: segmentation models like SAM
  • Audio notes: speech models
  • Trends over time: forecasting models

Before using AI, ask: What kind of input do I have? What kind of output do I need? What needs human review before I use it?

Use the AI Models page as a reference. You do not need to memorize the categories. The goal is to match the tool to the job.

2. Start with Safe Settings

A little background helps the AI respond more usefully, but do not give it private or confidential information.

Safe personalization example

I often use AI to draft clear workplace emails, summaries, checklists, and training materials. I prefer professional but plain English. Ask clarifying questions when details are missing.

Do not upload or paste

  • Payroll data, passwords, or access codes
  • Confidential customer data or company secrets
  • Private employee records or performance issues
  • Protected health data or legal records
  • Internal documents you are not allowed to share outside your organization

5. Build the Detailed Prompt with Halo Forge

Halo Forge can help build the detailed prompt faster. You can also write or edit the prompt manually.

Uploaded file analysis prompt

Analyze the attached files, including the CSV, notes, and SOP sample. Look for useful patterns, repeated issues, totals, trends, and anything helpful for a supervisor.

Do not invent facts.
If the data is unclear, tell me what is missing.
Show the most useful findings first.

Include:
- Most useful findings
- Totals or trends worth noticing
- Repeated issues
- Connections between the CSV, notes, and SOP sample
- What is missing or unclear
- Recommendations for a supervisor
- A short "what to verify" section

Keep the response practical, plain-English, and workplace appropriate.

What to check

Check totals, labels, date ranges, missing rows, unusual values, and whether the data actually supports the conclusion. AI can summarize a table, but it can also misread one.

Now use the prompt with the three prepared practice files.

Run the workflow

7. Create a Pre-Meeting Brief

A pre-meeting brief helps you walk into a conversation with the main facts, questions, and decisions in one place.

Second pass: brief from the analysis

After the first analysis, ask AI to turn the findings into a one-page pre-meeting brief.

Use the analysis above and the attached practice files to create a one-page pre-meeting brief.

Include:
- Purpose
- Key facts
- Open questions
- Risks
- Decisions needed
- Suggested next steps

Do not invent facts. Mark anything uncertain.

Compare sources

Compare these sources and identify:
- Where they agree
- Where they differ
- What seems unclear
- What should be verified before the meeting
- Questions I should ask

Keep the answer short and practical.

9. Review Before Use

AI output is a draft, not final truth.

Review and safety checklist

  • Remove private or sensitive data before uploading anything
  • Do not upload payroll, passwords, confidential customer data, private employee records, protected health data, or company secrets
  • Check names, dates, numbers, policies, and claims before sharing
  • Verify important facts against a trusted source
  • Make sure the tone fits your workplace and audience
  • Take responsibility for the final version before you send or post it
