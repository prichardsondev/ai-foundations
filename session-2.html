Session 2

Using AI for Everyday Work Tasks

A practical session showing how to use AI tools like ChatGPT for common workplace tasks — drafting emails, summarizing notes, rewriting instructions, and turning rough ideas into polished content.

What you'll take away

  • Use AI confidently for everyday writing and organizing tasks
  • Improve emails, notes, instructions, and announcements
  • Set up basic personalization and safe habits
  • Upload images or documents and get useful help from them
  • Create at least one polished work-related result during the hands-on lab
  • Understand simple privacy and review practices

What this session covers

  • Getting started with ChatGPT and finding key features
  • Basic settings and safe personalization
  • Everyday workplace tasks AI handles well
  • Better prompting for real work
  • Using uploads (images and documents)
  • Hands-on Lab: Turn a rough idea into polished output

1. Getting Started with ChatGPT

Before diving into tasks, spend a few minutes getting comfortable with the tool.

Quick start steps

Go to chatgpt.com and sign in with Google, Microsoft, or create a free account.

A free account is enough for everything we’ll cover today.

Key buttons to know

  • New chat – Start a fresh conversation
  • Settings – Usually behind your profile or gear icon
  • Attach / Upload – Add images, screenshots, or documents

Personalization

Inside Settings you can tell the tool about your role and preferred style. This helps it give better answers without you repeating yourself.

2. Safe Personalization & Settings

A little background helps the AI respond more usefully — but safety comes first.

Safe example you can use

I often use AI to draft clear workplace emails, summaries, and training materials. I prefer professional but plain English.

Privacy reminder

Never paste passwords, payroll data, private employee records, customer information, or confidential company data into AI tools.

Treat it as a helpful assistant, not a secure storage system.

3. Everyday Work Tasks AI Handles Well

Here are realistic tasks where AI can save you time.

Office & Knowledge Work

  • Drafting or rewriting professional emails
  • Summarizing meeting notes into clear bullets
  • Turning rough notes into readable updates
  • Creating short announcements or team messages
  • Organizing ideas or training plans

Shop-Floor & Operations

  • Rewriting instructions in plain English for new employees
  • Simplifying SOPs or procedures
  • Creating safety reminders or checklists
  • Turning verbal descriptions into written steps
  • Writing short shift or equipment updates

Important reality check

AI usually gives you a good starting draft — not a final product. Always review, adjust, and make it your own before sharing at work.

4. Better Prompting for Real Work

Clear prompts lead to much better results.

Helpful prompt ingredients

  • What you want the AI to do
  • Who the output is for
  • Key details or context
  • Tone and length preferences
  • Desired format (email, bullets, checklist, etc.)

Email example

Draft a clear professional email to my team letting them know Friday’s shift starts 30 minutes earlier than usual. Keep it under 100 words. Friendly but professional tone.

Rewrite example

Rewrite these instructions so a new employee can follow them easily. Use plain English, keep steps in order, and number each step. [paste instructions here]

5. Using Uploads (Images & Documents)

You can attach photos, screenshots, or documents and ask the AI to help you work with them.

Good things to upload

  • Photo of whiteboard notes or handwritten instructions
  • Screenshot of a form or procedure
  • Flyer or announcement you want to improve

Pro tip: Always add a clear question or task after uploading. For example: “Summarize this as five bullet points” or “Rewrite this so it’s easier for a new employee to follow.”

6. Hands-on Lab

Put what you’ve learned into practice. Turn one rough work idea into a polished result using AI.

Choose one task to work on

  • Improve a rough or unclear email
  • Summarize meeting or shift handoff notes
  • Rewrite a procedure or instructions for a new employee
  • Create a short workplace announcement or safety reminder
  • Build a simple one-page draft (welcome message, training note, shift update, etc.)

Starter prompt you can copy

I am new to AI tools. Help me with a common work task. Ask me a few simple questions one at a time if needed. Then help me create a polished result. My task is: [describe your task here, e.g. writing an email / summarizing notes / rewriting instructions]. Keep the language clear, professional, and easy to understand.

Tips for success: Start with something real (even messy notes are fine). Review the first output, ask for changes if needed, and iterate until you’re happy with the result.
