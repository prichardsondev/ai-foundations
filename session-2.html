Session 2

Using AI for Everyday Work Tasks

A practical, easy-to-follow session on using ChatGPT for common workplace tasks such as emails, summaries, drafting, rewriting, organizing ideas, and turning rough notes into polished content.

Session outcomes

  • Use ChatGPT for common workplace writing tasks
  • Improve rough notes, emails, and instructions
  • Understand basic settings, personalization, and useful core features
  • Upload an image or document and ask useful questions about it
  • Create one polished work-related result with AI help
  • Recognize simple privacy and review habits before sharing output

What this session covers

  • Setting up ChatGPT and finding core features
  • Basic settings and personalization
  • Safe use and what not to share
  • Everyday workplace prompting
  • Drafting and rewriting
  • Summaries and organizing rough notes
  • Uploading files or images for help
  • Turning AI output into a shareable work document

1. Getting started with ChatGPT

A straightforward walkthrough of the basics before you do anything else.

Key idea

Before using any new tool at work, it helps to spend a few minutes just looking around. ChatGPT is not complicated once you know where things are.

Creating or signing in to an account

Go to chatgpt.com and sign in with an existing Google or Microsoft account, or create a free account using your email address.

A free account gives you access to the core features used in this class. Paid plans add extras such as faster responses and more upload options, but you do not need a paid account to follow along today.

What to look for when you first open it

  • New chat — starts a fresh conversation
  • Settings — usually in a menu accessed through your account name or a gear icon
  • Personalization — found inside Settings, lets you give the tool background information about your role and preferences
  • Attach or upload button — lets you add a photo, document, or screenshot to your message
  • Model or tool options — some accounts show a dropdown to switch between different versions; if you do not see one, do not worry about it

A note on plans and features

Not every account looks the same. Some features, such as file uploads or advanced tools, may appear only on certain plan levels. If something described here looks different on your screen, that is normal. Focus on what is available to you and ask the instructor if you are unsure. You do not need every tool or paid feature to complete today's work successfully.

2. Personalization and basic settings

Telling the tool a little about yourself helps it give more useful answers.

What personalization does

In Settings, there is usually a Personalization section. It lets you tell ChatGPT things like your job role, the kind of tasks you use it for, or the tone you prefer. The tool uses this background information to shape its responses without you having to repeat yourself every time.

Depending on your account, the tool may also remember things from past conversations. This is called memory. You can turn it off if you prefer to start fresh each time.

A safe personalization example

Here is the kind of thing you can safely add to the personalization field:

I often use ChatGPT to help draft clear workplace emails and training materials.

This gives the tool enough context to be helpful without sharing anything sensitive.

Other safe things to include

  • Your general job area, such as manufacturing, logistics, HR, or administration
  • Your preferred tone, such as professional, clear, or plain English
  • Common tasks you want help with, such as writing emails or summarizing notes

Privacy reminder

Do not paste passwords, payroll data, private employee records, customer secrets, or any other sensitive information into ChatGPT. Even in the personalization section. Treat it like a public tool — useful for everyday tasks, but not a place for confidential data.

3. Everyday work tasks AI handles well

These are the kinds of tasks where a few minutes with ChatGPT can save significant time.

Key idea

AI is genuinely useful for writing tasks that involve drafting, organizing, or improving text. It is not a replacement for your judgment — but it is a solid starting point.

Office tasks

  • Drafting a professional email to a colleague, supervisor, or vendor
  • Rewriting a message to sound clearer or more polished
  • Summarizing meeting notes into a few short bullet points
  • Turning rough bullet points into a readable short update
  • Drafting a short workplace announcement
  • Organizing a training plan for the next few weeks

Shop-floor and operational tasks

  • Simplifying written instructions so a new employee can follow them
  • Rewriting an SOP in plain language
  • Drafting a short safety reminder for the team
  • Turning a rough verbal description into a short written procedure
  • Creating a quick checklist from a list of steps
  • Writing a brief update to a supervisor about a shift or equipment issue

Realistic expectations

ChatGPT produces a useful starting draft most of the time. Expect to read it, adjust a few things, and make it your own before using it at work. That review step is not extra work — it is the habit that keeps AI useful and safe.

4. Better prompting for real work

You do not need a complicated system. A clear prompt usually includes a few basic pieces.

What to include in a useful prompt

  • What you want — the task you are asking for
  • Who it is for — the audience or recipient
  • Important details — the key facts the tool needs
  • Tone or length — how it should sound and how long it should be
  • Format — how the answer should be laid out

Why this matters

When prompts are vague, the tool fills in the blanks on its own — and you may not like the result. A little more detail upfront usually saves time in the long run.

Email example

Prompt

Draft a clear professional email to my team letting them know Friday's shift starts 30 minutes earlier than usual. Keep it under 100 words. Friendly but professional tone.

Summary example

Prompt

Summarize these meeting notes into five short bullet points for staff. Keep each point to one sentence. Plain, clear language.

[paste your notes here]

Rewrite or instruction example

Prompt

Rewrite these instructions so a new employee can follow them easily. Use plain English. Keep the steps in order. Number each step.

[paste your instructions here]

5. Uploads: using an image or document

You can attach real materials to your message and ask ChatGPT to help you work with them.

What you can upload

Depending on your account, you can attach photos, screenshots, flyers, handwritten notes, or document images directly to your message. This is useful when you have something real to work from rather than starting from scratch.

What to upload

  • A photo of a whiteboard or handwritten notes
  • A screenshot of a form or procedure
  • A flyer or announcement you want to improve
  • A document image you want summarized
  • A workplace sign or label you want rewritten

What to ask after uploading

  • "What do you see in this image?"
  • "What seems most important here?"
  • "Turn this into a short email."
  • "Summarize this as five bullet points."
  • "Rewrite this so it is easier for a new employee to follow."

Keep it practical

Uploads work best when you tell the tool what you want it to do with the material. Just uploading an image without a question usually gives a vague response. Add a clear task and you will get a much more useful result.

6. Hands-on lab

Turn one rough work idea into a polished result.

Choose one task that is close to something you actually do at work. Use ChatGPT to take it from rough to ready.

If you are unsure where to begin, start with an email or short summary. These are usually the easiest first tasks.

Pick one task to work on

  • Improve a rough or unclear email
  • Summarize notes from a meeting or shift handoff
  • Rewrite a procedure or set of instructions for a new employee
  • Create a short workplace announcement
  • Build a simple one-page draft such as a safety reminder, a welcome page for new employees, a training announcement, a team event page, or a shift update

Starter prompt to copy

If you are not sure how to start, copy this and paste it into ChatGPT:

I am new to AI tools. Help me use ChatGPT for a common work task. Ask me a few simple questions one at a time. Then help me create a polished result. My task is: [writing an email / summarizing notes / rewriting instructions / creating a simple workplace page]. Keep the language clear, professional, and easy to understand.

Replace the bracketed text with your actual task, then let the tool guide you.

Tips for a good lab session

  • Start with something real — even rough notes or a half-written email works well
  • Read the first result and ask for a change if something is off
  • Try adjusting the tone, length, or format and see what happens
  • When you get a result you like, copy it somewhere to keep

7. Creating a shareable document

How to turn AI-generated text into something you can actually hand to someone.

A simple recommended workflow

Start simple. You do not need to learn special formatting codes or export settings. Here is a straightforward path that works for most workplace documents.

Quick review before sharing

  • Is it accurate?
  • Is the tone right for my audience?
  • Did I remove anything sensitive?
  • Is it ready to send or share?

Step by step

  1. Ask the AI to generate clear, formatted text with headings and bullet points in plain text — not code, not special symbols
  2. Read through the result and ask for any changes before you move it anywhere
  3. Copy the final text and paste it into Word or Google Docs
  4. Clean up the formatting in the document and add any finishing touches
  5. Save as PDF only at the end if you need it in that format

Training plan example prompt

Copy and adjust this for your own use

Help me create a professional training plan for [topic] over the next [number] weeks. Ask me a few simple questions first. Then create a clear, well-formatted document in plain text with headings, short sections, and bullet points. Include the purpose, audience, timeline, weekly topics, activities, needed materials, and expected outcomes.

Why start with plain text

Plain formatted text is easy to copy, easy to edit, and works in any word processor. Special formatting codes and symbols can cause problems when you paste into a document. Keeping it simple at the start saves cleanup time later.

8. Common early mistakes

Most early problems come from the same few habits. These are easy to fix once you know what to watch for.

Prompt mistakes

  • Being too vague about what you need
  • Forgetting to name the audience
  • Not asking for a specific format
  • Giving too many instructions at once

Usage mistakes

  • Trusting the first answer too quickly
  • Pasting sensitive or confidential information
  • Skipping a basic read-through before sharing
  • Sending AI-written text without reviewing it for your actual audience

9. Key reminders

A short summary of the most important ideas to remember from this session.

What to remember

  • AI is useful for drafting, rewriting, and organizing — but you still need to review the result.
  • Better prompts usually lead to better output.
  • Uploads can help you turn real materials into useful results without starting from scratch.
  • Start with plain formatted text when creating shareable documents.
  • Always review before sending or sharing anything AI helped you write.
  • Do not share sensitive information — treat ChatGPT like a public tool.
