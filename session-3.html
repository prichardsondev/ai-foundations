Session 3

Using Projects to Build with AI

This session is about moving from single prompts to building something with AI inside one project.

Session outcomes

  • Explain what a project is in simple terms
  • Recognize project-style workspaces across tools like ChatGPT and Microsoft 365 Copilot
  • Understand why chats drift without structure
  • Use a short session brief to keep work aligned
  • See one strong planning prompt
  • Understand the high-level workflow from plan to publish

What this session covers

  • What a project is and why it helps
  • How project-style workspaces show up across AI tools
  • Why separate chats can drift away from your goal
  • How a short session brief keeps work consistent
  • One strong example and one strong planning prompt
  • The simple workflow from plan to publish

1. What is a Project?

A project is a workspace for one goal.

Simple definition

A project keeps your chats, instructions, and files together in one place.

Think of it like one workspace for one job. Instead of starting over each time, you keep everything connected.

2. Project workspaces across AI tools

Different tools use different names, but the workflow pattern is similar.

Different AI tools use different names for project-style workspaces. ChatGPT calls them Projects. Microsoft 365 Copilot may use Notebooks, Pages, or Agents depending on what you are doing and what your organization has enabled.

The important idea is the same:

  • Keep the goal in one place
  • Keep useful files and context nearby
  • Reuse instructions instead of starting from scratch
  • Keep related chats or outputs connected
  • Review the final result before sharing or using it
Tool / Feature What it is useful for
ChatGPT Projects Keeping chats, files, and project instructions together around one goal
Copilot Notebooks Gathering work files, chats, meeting notes, pages, and links for a focused task, when available depending on your license and organization settings
Copilot Pages Turning Copilot responses into editable, shareable working content, when available depending on your license and organization settings
Copilot Agents Creating or using specialized helpers for repeated work or business processes, when available depending on your license and organization settings

Copilot note

In Microsoft 365 Copilot, you may see options like Notebooks, Pages, Agents, Files, Emails, People, and Meetings. These are workplace ways to organize or reference context. The names are different, but the habit is the same: give AI a clear goal, useful context, and reusable instructions.

3. The Problem: Chats Drift

Without structure, different chats can lose context and consistency.

One chat may sound clear and simple. Another may change the tone, miss the audience, or format the result differently.

That is why projects need a small amount of structure before you start.

4. The Solution: A Session Brief

A short set of instructions tells AI what you are building and how to help.

Example

A goal says what you want to make. A brief says how AI should help. A prompt is the message you type to start the work.

# Session Brief

Goal:
Create a one-page guide that explains an idea, solves a small problem, or helps someone complete a task

Audience:
Non-technical users

Tone:
Simple, clear, step-by-step

Output:
Markdown document

Rules:
- Keep it simple
- Use bullet points
- Avoid technical terms
- Ask questions if something is unclear

You write this once and reuse it across all chats.

5. Mini project activity

Choose one small thing you could actually build or organize with AI this week.

Examples

  • A one-page guide for a workplace task
  • A checklist for a repeated process
  • A short training handout
  • A meeting follow-up template
  • A simple spreadsheet analysis summary
  • A personal study guide
  • A customer email response pattern

Then answer

  1. What is the goal?
  2. Who is the audience?
  3. What files, notes, or examples would help?
  4. What style should the AI use?
  5. What does "done" look like?

Project brief prompt

I want to create a small AI project.

Goal:
[what I want to build]

Audience:
[who this is for]

Useful context:
[files, notes, examples, emails, spreadsheets, or links I can provide]

Style:
[clear, professional, friendly, step-by-step, short, detailed, etc.]

Done means:
[what the finished result should look like]

Help me turn this into a short project brief I can reuse.

6. Why This Works

A brief gives every chat the same starting point.

It keeps tone, structure, and output consistent across chats.

Instead of repeating yourself in every new conversation, you reuse the same brief and stay aligned.

7. Example Planning Prompt

Use the brief when you begin planning.

What to do right now

First, copy your session brief. Next, open a new chat. Then paste the brief and use the planning prompt below.

Here is my session brief:

[paste your brief here]

Help me plan this project.

Ask me questions one at a time, then help me create a clear outline before writing anything.
