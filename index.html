Who this is for

No technical background required.

Workplace roles

This course is for employees across production, quality, maintenance, engineering, scheduling, procurement, finance, HR, office staff, and leadership.

Why it matters

AI can save time and improve consistency, but only if used correctly. The course focuses on simple, safe habits that help learners get value quickly without hype.

What you will learn

Keep it practical, useful, and ready for daily work.

Core topics

  • What AI is and is not
  • How to get better results by asking better questions
  • Quick verification habits
  • Real workplace examples from shop and office settings

Everyday work wins

  • Emails, writing, and summaries
  • SOPs, checklists, and training materials
  • Spreadsheet and document support
  • Reusable prompts and templates for your role

What you leave with

Practical tools learners can use the same day.

Learner takeaways

  • Reusable AI prompts and templates
  • A what-not-to-share safety checklist
  • Confidence using AI right away
  • Shared vocabulary across departments

Prerequisites

  • At least 18 years of age
  • High school diploma, GED, or HiSet
  • Resident of Maine
  • Proof of authorization to work in the U.S.

How this site was built

A practical example of the same AI workflow this course teaches.

This course site is also a real-world example of practical AI use. It was planned with ChatGPT, built with Codex, reviewed with Claude, and finalized by a human instructor. Each tool helped with a different part of the work, but none of them replaced judgment. That is one of the core ideas of this class: use AI to assist, improve, and accelerate work — not to turn your brain off.
