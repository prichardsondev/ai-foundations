Microsoft 365 Copilot at Work

Copilot is not just a chatbot. In Microsoft 365, it can connect to work tools like Outlook, OneDrive, Excel, Word, PowerPoint, and Teams depending on your organization's settings and license.

Why this matters

You may already see Copilot at work, or you may encounter it soon. The goal is not to memorize every Microsoft feature. The goal is to understand what Copilot can do, how to prompt it clearly, and how to use it responsibly in real workplace situations.

When available, Copilot can help you draft messages, summarize documents, organize notes, explain spreadsheet data, and turn rough information into a more useful starting point. You still need to check the result, protect sensitive information, and follow your employer's rules.

These are practical work habits you can recognize, try, and reuse when Copilot is available at work.

Use / to reference work context

In Microsoft 365 Copilot Chat, typing / in the prompt box can open available work references. Depending on your license, organization settings, and Copilot mode, this may include files, people, meetings, or emails. This is sometimes called Context IQ.

The / shortcut is how you tell Copilot, "Use this specific work item as context." Instead of asking a vague question, you can point Copilot at a specific file, email, meeting, or person when those options are available.

Feature availability varies. Some users may only be able to reference files. Others may see files, people, meetings, or emails depending on Microsoft 365 Copilot licensing and organization settings.

Key idea

This is one of the biggest differences between a generic chatbot and workplace Copilot. The value is not just the model. The value is the model plus the work context you intentionally provide.

Set up email drafting instructions

In Outlook, when available, Copilot can use custom drafting instructions for email. This lets you tell Copilot how you want email drafts to sound before it writes them.

This is useful when you repeatedly ask Copilot to make emails shorter, more direct, warmer, more formal, or organized with bullet points. Feature availability depends on your license and organization settings.

How to find it

  1. Open new Outlook.
  2. Select the Copilot menu or Copilot icon, if it is available in your Outlook version.
  3. Choose Settings.
  4. Choose Draft Instructions.
  5. Turn on "Use custom instructions when drafting email."
  6. Add your email-writing preferences.
  7. Save.

Example instructions

  • Keep emails short, clear, and professional.
  • Use a friendly but direct tone.
  • Use bullet points when listing tasks or next steps.
  • Avoid overly formal language unless I ask for it.
  • End with a clear action item or next step.
  • Sign emails with my first name.

Key idea

This is similar to giving Copilot a reusable writing agreement. Instead of fixing every email after Copilot drafts it, you can teach Copilot your preferred email style once and adjust it later.

Example prompts

Use these as starting points, then add details about audience, format, tone, and source material.

Everyday work prompts

  • Summarize the main points in this document.
  • Draft a professional reply to this email.
  • Draft a short professional reply using my email style.
  • Rewrite this email so it is clear, friendly, and action-oriented.
  • Create a checklist from these notes.
  • Find trends in this spreadsheet.
  • Explain this formula in plain English.
  • Create a chart that shows monthly totals.
  • Schedule this prompt to run every Monday morning.

Context reference prompts

  • Summarize /Project Plan for the team.
  • Compare /Budget Draft with /Final Budget.
  • Draft a follow-up email based on /Monday Client Meeting.
  • Create a checklist from /Training Notes.
  • What are the main action items from /Team Meeting?

Try this in Excel

Excel is often the easiest place to show the value of Copilot because you can see formulas, charts, summaries, and analysis inside a familiar tool. Instead of only reading a chat answer, you can compare Copilot's suggestions against the spreadsheet itself.

Try asking Copilot to summarize a table, identify patterns, explain a formula, suggest a chart, or describe what changed between two sets of numbers. Then slow down and verify the result. Check whether the data range is correct, whether the chart answers the real question, and whether the explanation matches the spreadsheet.

Safe workplace use

Important warning

Work Copilot may have enterprise protections, but you should still follow employer policy. Do not paste sensitive, private, regulated, or confidential data into AI tools unless your organization allows it.

Before you use an AI result

  • Check the facts, numbers, names, dates, and source material.
  • Make sure the tone fits the workplace situation.
  • Confirm that you are allowed to use the data in the prompt.
  • Use human review before sending, publishing, or making decisions.

Availability note

If your account does not show a feature, that does not mean you did anything wrong. Copilot capabilities depend on licensing, admin settings, app access, and where you are working.

Closing takeaway

Copilot is most useful when you stop treating it like a magic chatbot and start treating it like a work assistant that needs clear instructions, useful context, and human review.
