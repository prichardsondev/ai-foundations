# Draft Training Outline

Working title: Safe and Smart AI at Work

## Rough agenda

1. Welcome and what AI is
2. Large language models
3. Neural networks
4. Tokens and context windows
5. Prompting basics
6. Better prompting with role, task, context, format, and constraints
7. Prompt examples
8. Prompt mistakes
9. Privacy
10. Uploaded files
11. Privacy again, but with examples
12. Company policy
13. Email drafting
14. Email tone
15. Email review
16. Spreadsheets
17. CSV files
18. Formulas and hallucinations
19. AI agents
20. APIs
21. Automation
22. MCP
23. When not to use AI
24. Bias
25. Copyright
26. Accessibility
27. Job replacement fears
28. Hands-on activity
29. Questions
30. Wrap-up

## Good ideas worth keeping

- Use a fictional email instead of a real customer message.
- Include a checklist for "Should I use AI for this?"
- Show a before-and-after prompt.
- Ask participants to identify sensitive information in a fake example.
- Give managers a short follow-up checklist.

## Problems with this draft

- Too many topics for 60 minutes.
- Some sections are repeated.
- Some sections are too technical for this audience.
- The activity is not defined yet.
- There is not enough time reserved for questions.
- It does not clearly connect AI use to everyday work.

## Possible activity

Give staff a rough email and ask them to write a safer prompt. Then compare what information should be included and what should be left out.
