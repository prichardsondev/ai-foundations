# Training Request

## Request summary

Priya Raman, Operations Manager for Northstar Community Services, is requesting a 60-minute staff training session called "Using AI Safely at Work."

## Audience

- 24 to 30 staff members
- Mix of managers, coordinators, administrative assistants, trainers, and team leads
- Most are comfortable with email, shared drives, and spreadsheets
- Only a few have used AI tools more than a handful of times
- No coding background expected

## Goals

- Help staff understand what AI can and cannot do well
- Give staff a safe way to use AI for everyday work tasks
- Practice writing clearer prompts
- Explain when not to use AI
- Show how to review AI output before sharing it
- Reduce anxiety about AI replacing jobs by focusing on judgment, review, and responsible use

## Constraints

- 60 minutes total
- Must be practical and plain-English
- No live use of private employee, client, vendor, or financial data
- Use fictional examples only
- Include one short hands-on activity
- Avoid jargon unless it is explained simply
- Leave at least 5 minutes for questions

## Tone

Calm, practical, respectful, and beginner-friendly. Avoid hype. Avoid fear-based messaging.

## Time limit

60 minutes, including discussion and questions.

## Topics that must be included

- Safe prompting basics
- Privacy and uploaded files
- Checking AI answers
- When not to use AI
- Using AI for email drafts
- Using AI for spreadsheet summaries
- How AI can support work without replacing human judgment

## Topics to avoid or keep light

- Coding
- Building custom agents
- Model architecture
- API setup
- Vendor comparisons
- Deep legal or compliance detail
