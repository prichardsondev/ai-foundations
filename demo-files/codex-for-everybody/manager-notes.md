# Manager Notes

Notes from Priya Raman after a planning call.

## Clear priorities

- Keep the training useful for non-technical staff.
- Spend real time on privacy and uploaded files.
- Include examples for email, spreadsheets, and meeting notes.
- Make sure people know they must check AI answers before using them.
- Include a simple "when not to use AI" checklist.
- Avoid using any real staff or client data in the demo.

## Concerns

- Some staff are pasting too much into free AI tools without thinking about privacy.
- A few people treat AI answers as finished work instead of a first draft.
- Managers worry staff may use AI for sensitive HR situations without review.
- Finance staff want help with spreadsheets but are worried AI may invent numbers.
- Customer Support wants email help but does not want replies that sound fake or too cheerful.

## Vague comments that need clarification

- "Make it inspiring, but not too much."
- "We should probably mention policy, but I do not know which policy yet."
- "Maybe include something about agents if there is time."
- "Some teams need more detail than others."
- "Do not scare people, but make the risks clear."

## Possible success measures

- Staff can name three kinds of information they should not paste into AI.
- Staff can write one clear prompt for an everyday task.
- Staff can explain why AI output needs review.
- Staff can identify at least two tasks where AI should not be used without approval.

## Open questions

- Which internal AI policy should be referenced?
- Are staff allowed to use public AI tools, approved workplace tools only, or both?
- Should the session include a manager-only follow-up?
- Who will approve the final handout?
